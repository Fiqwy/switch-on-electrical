2-minute check

Is your home electrically safe?

Five quick questions. We score what we find against current QLD standards and tell you what (if anything) needs attention. No sales push.

How old is your switchboard?

A glance at the fuse box gives it away — ceramic fuses = old, plastic safety switches with test buttons = newer.

Do your safety switches still trip when you test them monthly?

There's a test button on each safety switch — pressing it should cut power to that circuit immediately.

Are your smoke alarms interconnected and hard-wired?

QLD requires interconnected photoelectric alarms in every bedroom and hallway. If one goes off, all of them go off.

Do you have halogen downlights still in the ceiling?

Halogens run extremely hot and are a common cause of insulation fires. They also burn through power.

When was the home last inspected by a licensed electrician?

Older homes (pre-2000) should get a full safety check every 5–10 years, and at change of ownership.
